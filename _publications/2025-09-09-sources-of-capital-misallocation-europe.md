This paper investigates the sources of capital misallocation across 19 European countries using the framework of David and Venkateswaran (2019). Using firm-level data, the paper decomposes misallocation into persistent distortions, adjustment frictions and transitory factors. The results show that persistent distortions account for most observed capital misallocation, although the relative importance of different sources varies across countries and industries.
